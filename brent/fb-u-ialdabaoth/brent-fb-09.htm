Oliver Habryka I think LessWrong should preserve the functionality of links, and it's likely we will make the comments visible on the posts Duncan moved to his drafts. I don't think the posts should be visible from his profile or from the frontpage (I.e. should only be accessible via link).

I think I want to get to a stage where at a certain level of curation, we can guarantee that the content stays up on the site. Of Duncans posts, the Double Crux post was I think the only one that obviously met that bar, and Duncan did restore it after me pinging him about it. But to do this more reliably, we want to get more transparent consent from authors that they are fine with their content being up on LW forever, which we currently don't get, and I don't yet have a fully formed model what the exact threshold is after which we want to guarantee access to the content. Probably everything in curated is a good start.

Bears repeating:

I will tell you how you make people's lives meaningful again.

1. You give everyone a basic income guarantee....
2. You reduce all intellectual property rights down to reasonable spans of 5 to 15 years, depending on the kind of IP.
3. You make computers, 3d printers, editing software, video recorders, audio mixing tools, animation suites, and the like cheap cheap cheap, and easy enough for little kids to use.
4. You work on improving tools like Wikipedia, Khan Academy, craigslist, and {please-someone-make-something-better-than-Reddit}.
5. You open up more public land for art projects, hiking, public works, shared community gardens, museums, and the like.

Now people can share in the building of their culture, without being sued into oblivion. People can find meaning in building and sharing experiences.

Capitalism isn't the problem. Top-down consumerism is the problem. We need to move out of a command-driven consumer culture, and into a participatory culture. And yes, humans have it in us to do this.

You do this, and I won't need a Singularity to be happy. I won't have achieved arete, but I can live contentedly within that world.
