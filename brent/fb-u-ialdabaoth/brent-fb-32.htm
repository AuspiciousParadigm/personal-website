So. D&D classes.

Fighter, Rogue, Mage, Priest.

Then you expand out into 4th edition's role system and you get {warrior, knight, ranger, marshal}, {assassin, acrobat, thief, bard}, {evoker, abjurer, conjurer, artificer}, {zealot, paladin, theurge, cleric}.

...

So if we give each class a standardized progression schema, and use 4th Ed's excellent daily/encounter feat distinction, what makes the most sense?

I rather like 5ths progression pacing. First level should be pretty basic, with not too many overwhelming choices. You should get the chance to play a basic version of your class before you're suddenly thrust into the optimizer's dilemma.

So how about:

Level one - pick a class (fighter/rogue/mage/priest), get the class's basic class feature, get one daily or encounter feat and one at-will feat.

Level two - pick a role (striker/defender/controller/leader), get. a role-based class feature and a second at-will feat.

Level three - pick a subclass type thingy.

Need to flesh this all out more, back later.

Things to think about when starting a new relationship:

Love, Trust, and Desire.

How much of each do you need?

...

Lately, I tend to expect relationships with either a lot of Love and Trust, but no Desire, or a lot of Love and Desire, but no Trust. I intuitively feel like Trust and Desire play off each other in most of my potential relationships, primarily because I'm optimizing hard for Love.

I've occasionally tried to build relationships out of Trust and Desire without Love, but I wind up messing up when my desire for Love slips in unawares, and violates the Trust that the other person had in me that Love wouldn't become a factor.

I have a REALLY hard time with Trust, which means that Trust breakdowns tend to be the way most of my relationships fail. I don't always experience them as Trust breakdowns from the inside, of course; my first sense is that my partner doesn't Love or Desire me as much anymore. (Often this is true, but reacting to it as true won't help.) But once I start feeling a lessening of their Love or Desire, I start Trusting them less - and then, they start Trusting me less. And then we just spiral down until the whole thing falls apart.

If I could Trust someone to Love and Desire me, even when they actually don't, I could probably maintain my relationships for longer - they'd be at a net-negative, but they wouldn't end in such devastating spirals.
