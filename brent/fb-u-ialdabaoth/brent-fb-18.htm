Okay, so. This thing:

https://medium.com/@Th…/in-defense-of-punch-bug-68fcec56cd6b

Before I really dive into it, is there a word for the particular kind of failure of rationalism, where {social motivation X} prevents people from talking about {social motivation X}?

...

Because there's this thing that REALLY frustrates me about microaggressions, which is that they're actually important to track, but they're also MICRO-aggressions. Treating each one, individually, as a full aggression is fucking STUPID.

And people are reacting to the article in tragically predictable ways, treating Duncan's advocacy of micro-aggressions *in explicit contexts* as if he were advocating for ALL aggressions, ALL the time.

And apparently you can't say something like "hey guys, I think micro-aggressions are actually useful sometimes" without people literally trying to find reasons why you're evil. (Witness: https://www.facebook.com/durandal42/posts/10101087360522628 )

The problem here is that our culture wants to treat personal sovereignty and body integrity as a Sacred Value, but we as a culture have not yet fully specified the boundaries around its sacredness, and have not yet worked out the shape of that sacredness in a logically consistent manner. So we're demanding that people hold to a set of logically conflicting taboos, which inevitably means that those with power get a free pass and those without power get attacked as if they were a powerful force against sacredness.

Which, you know... way to go, guys.

The only way around this mess is to ditch the whole concept of sacredness, and examine the situation dispassionately. Look at agents attempting to achieve their goals in a potentially competitive environment, and figure out where the attractors are. You know, Robin Hanson stuff.

https://www.overcomingbias.com/…/why-economics-is-and-shoul…

... Goddamnit, guys.

Okay. But surely Scott Alexander could explain hot-button concepts without getting into massive trouble, right? I mean, just look at how he treated the incel thing:

http://slatestarcodex.com/…/31/radicalizing-the-romanceless/

... Jesus christ.

The point I'm trying to make, here, is that it doesn't matter how gently you point at these topics; these are Sacred Values, and they're logically incoherent, and so pointing at them AT ALL allows you to be attacked if you smell at all outgroup. Because there's literally nothing intelligent you can say about them that won't hit at least ONE side of the inherent paradox.

Which I think is exactly how our culture wants it. It masks what the *real* value is ("be powerful and liked") behind a fake value ("be kind to the 'less powerful', don't be creepy, and don't be privileged and entitled"), such that clueless people who pursue the fake value will never successfully compete with the people who have already achieved the real value.

Anyway. With that out of the way, I'll dive into the article itself in detail sometime in the next week or two.
