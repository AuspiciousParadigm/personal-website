I think, deep down, the spiritual cancer that's eating away at me is that I don't feel Valuable.

I know, deep down, that Value is actually an economic concept. It means making choices, choosing between options, choosing the option that is "higher value". Or, looking at it the other way, Value is the property of *being* the more-preferred choice.

People tell me all the time how valuable my drive, my creativity, my intelligence, and my caring are. But when they have a chance to... choose between me and someone with a good physique, they choose the physique. The physique has Value; comparatively, my talents and relationship do not.

Or when they have a chance to choose between me and someone with social credit, they choose the social credit. The social credit has Value; comparatively, my talents and relationship do not.

Or when they have a chance to choose between me and someone with money or financial capital or other material resources, they choose the resources. The resources have Value; comparatively, my talents and relationship do not.

Or when they have the chance to choose between me and an old friend, they choose the friend. That friendship has Value; comparatively, my talents and relationship do not.

And then they try to say, "it's not that I don't value you, it's just that..."

No, bitch. You don't Value me. THAT'S WHAT THE WORD MEANS. Your actions are precisely following the definition of the word. It is INSULTING for you to try to convince me that you Value me with words, while your actions continue to contribute to this state.

And they glare angrily, and say "well fine, what would Value you?", as if they don't already know, and as if it's MY responsibility to tell them. And I look them in the eye and say "if you really Valued me, you'd treat me the way you treat people you Value. Which means YOU, the buyer, would make an offer. And you would respect my choice whether to accept that offer, based on how much I Value what you're offering. You wouldn't throw it in my face and demand that I tell you what I need, so that you can tell me that it's unreasonable while still demanding that I pretend that you Value me."

And then someone else, who chooses me more often than not, and who has been there when it was hard, says "hey, I Value you."

And I say "yeah, yeah you do. But you don't have the capital to pay the upkeep cost. Which sucks. Believe me, I know it sucks. But I need to be Valued by enough people to pay the upkeep costs, which actually by my computation are WAY less than that guy over there who everyone Values for way less than I put in, so I dunno what gives."

And then my friend looks sadly at the situation, and sort of resigns to slowly losing me.

So sad.

Lauren Chris Horne this meshes really well with stuff I've been thinking recently about status being the wrong question! Because you really are *a kind of* valuable to these people. The question is not how high status a person is, the question is how valuable is a unit of interacting with the person for various modes and purposes. The thing one is inclined to call status is commodities speculation on the value of these interactions. People don't keep *bank accounts* in their heads, it's not zero sum like that - people keep *value predictor*, trying to guess what the future value of units of interaction of different types will be.

Interacting with you in the "download your knowledge" mode has been very valuable for a bunch of people, but hanging out with you in the "friends hanging out" mode feels difficult and dragging, I think just because of fairly normal things in the social anxiety/depression cluster. You can feel how your emotions trade low even though some of your skills trade high.

This integrates why Being Ok is a good hack: people feel like interacting with you is more valuable when you shut off "suffering"/"attempted telekinesis" about your feeling bad, because then you're not charging *them* a fee per second they don't fix your problem. This is why someone saying "hey, please avoid person x" is scary: they're selling your commodity for very low, which might make other traders update. This is why the pattern in rationalist awkward crowd folks happens of "I try to be so moral but nobody wants to hang out with me, what's going on?" happens: they're conflating different types of value, where people want the value of a person being moral and accepting *to them*.

And it seems like what's going on for you is that you are stuck in a situation where you really crave the value of people interacting with you honestly, but have a very hard time providing this value without charging fees like "I'm very anxious and lonely and you haven't fixed that completely", and "I'm not convinced this is actually real and a relationship I can rely on". This *sucks*, and *really is terrible*, and it's not right that you get to be stuck like this. It's a pattern a bunch of people around here have, but you have the interesting extra thing that you're - high status along one of the dimensions, I think? I'm pretty sure that's the true way to phrase it - but you're very anxious and validation-craving for your otherwise higher position. And you feel anxiety that your currency will lose value if you don't present as having connections to the High Status People.

I propose that your currency is not a tenth as volatile or vulnerable as your intuition thinks it is. it's just not currently highly directly valuable to people who don't already care about you (aka value it intrinsically) because of your stronger need for validation. I also propose that a *real* Brent donation box - one that has instructions for how to actually donate the right currency - might actually make an impact for you.
