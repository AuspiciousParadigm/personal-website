Sophia Streiffer In the three spatial dimensions, you can deposit at any branch and withdraw from any other branch without concern, transfer from one account to another is usually pretty easy. But if you saw that someone transferred 53 million from one account to another, while you could determine that person is *wealthy* you wouldn't conclude that *wealth* is *the amount transferred between accounts*. The thermodynamic arrow of time means that pastward and futureward behave somewhat differently. They don't know precisely how much he might deposit in the future, so they can't just do that amount minus the 53 million like they would if the deposit was pastward of now. So they track the 53 million explicitly. Similarly, he *might* not deposit in the future, and they have to charge extra to cover that, which they do as part of the "interest rate". But this is just a quirk of how the bank interacts with entropy, and largely an implementation detail. The general structure of Kayne West (assuming the bank judged correctly), is quite wealthy, and can afford to withdraw 53 million at one location in spacetime.
