Eowyn Dean Possible alternate solution: break it down into manageable sections of your experience or steps you need to follow and reach out to those people you really want to speak to. But limit it to one of those items per person per exchange. Limit the the length of the exchange. And make sure you and the other person understand at the end what you gained from it.

Knowing that you were able to help someone (to any extent) is what keeps talking to a depressed person from feeling futile (ie. emotionally exhausting). And knowing that they will respect your time even though it's difficult to stop when there's so much to deal with makes it more possible to accept the initial bid.

(Source: personal experience needing this type of help, then giving it to the point of exhaustion, then learning how to make giving it sustainable. No idea if it fits your needs, but figure it's worth sharing in case.)
