Daniel Powell If biology is a real thing, and we should expect it to matter whenever we find such a weak connection, we expect too many contradictory things from biology.

Given that women aren't in control of whether or not they are raped (an assumption that I make for the sake of the discussion without endorsing or rejecting), wouldn't it be pro-survival to not have any trauma at all?

Conversely, if people are in complete control of whether or not they are raped (Which is not the complement of the above assumption), it is nonsensical to have trauma associated with that either.

In the middle case, where people have some level of control over their autonomy, trauma over things that aren't fatality risks is antiadaptive.

The adaptive form of trauma is where it causes caution that avoids a repeat of a dangerous experience. Being sensitive enough to flag all actually dangerous experiences means flagging quite a few things that aren't fatality risks as dangerous - dogs that have learned food aggression behavior, for example, identify children petting them at mealtimes to be an existential threat. Bullied children, even when their lives aren't threatened, respond as though they were. And people are often afraid of high places regardless of whether or not they are securely anchored.

I'm currently trying to build a game, where each round has Prisoner's Dilemma or Ultimatum elements, but there are different kinds of "points" you are competing for, and you don't determine their relative values against each other until later in the game.

Basic sketched-out structure thus far:

You have four resources: red triangles, yellow stars, blue circles, and green squares. Each round, you pair up with another player and attempt to earn or trade resources, via various g...ames with different payoff matrices. Each player has a publicly available record of what moves they've made in previous rounds.

As you accumulate resources, you can expend them to influence who you partner with in future rounds.

As the game progresses, you can also expend resources to cause certain resources to be 'worth' more victory points in the final analysis, and can also expend resources to cause the end game to be collaborative or competitive.

I'm still working out the kinks, but expect to have something playable within a few days.
