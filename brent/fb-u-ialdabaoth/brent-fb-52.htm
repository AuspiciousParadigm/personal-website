Something to understand about the difference between the Astral, the Material and the Umbral:

You can't "create" anything Astral. What would it even *mean* to "invent" a new number? It only barely makes sense to say that you've "discovered" a new prime - it was out there, the whole time; all you did was instantiate a lower-complexity pointer to it in Material reality.

This extends to even larger-complexity structures. You cannot "invent" a new algorithm; all algorithms 'exist...' in the Astral, as necessary consequence of the initial definitional rules. They can only be 'discovered', in the sense that a lower-complexity pointer can be instantiated from Material space and labeled with "use this algorithm to accomplish these goals".

Likewise, nothing in Material reality can be 'created' or 'destroyed', only manipulated into new forms - and those forms are unchangingly Astral. Material reality is where Astral forms are imperfectly instantiated, so that "real work" can get done.

Actual creation is entirely a property of the Umbra.

In the Umbra, concepts like "new" and "undiscovered" and "mine" are Real and have Meaning.

When you "create" something "new", you're taking a form from the Astral (which has existed for as long as numbers have existed), instantiating an imperfect copy of it in the Material, and then imbuing it with Umbral meaning - ownership, pride, newness, and so on.

There's a lot of important detail in that statement.

Brent Dill ALSO remember that Astral, Umbral, and Material aren't separate "realms" per se; they're separate 'ways of being' in the world.

You can look at a branching structure thrusting up out of the ground and notice that it's a collection of wood and water with a certain hardness and flexibility and size and shape and color - that's the Material interpretation.
