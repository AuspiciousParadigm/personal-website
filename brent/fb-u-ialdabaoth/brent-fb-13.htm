Brent Dill Not necessarily. Material goods can be utilized towards multiple ends, many of which have greater net utility than others. And many of which can be used to acquire larger quantities of the goods, seemingly without end.

For example: we could use a fraction of all the palladium, platinum, gold, etc. on earth towards building robotic rockets, which could bring us back a single asteroid with more palladium, platinum, gold, etc. in it than every trace that has ever been mined on earth, tenfold. But if it's hard to do that because of how expensive they are, then it doesn't matter how cheap they could be when we've finished.

(granted, fuel costs are also prohibitive, but mostly because of energy costs, which could also come way down once we had enough palladium/platinum/etc. to work with)
