Mike Johnson This gets into fairly hairy metaphysical questions like, "what is real?"

The rationalist memeplex seems to suggest: beliefs, desires, value-functions, and agents are real. And the way these things are interwoven defines who we are, and what matters to us (cf the Complexity of Value Thesis).

This metaphysics, as you note, doesn't handle recursion very well. The question, "if you could edit your utility function, should you?" does seem to lead to a paradox.

There are alternative metaphysics/memeplexes which do not suffer from this recursive paradox.
