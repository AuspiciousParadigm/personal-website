Sealing Away The Gods (1/???)

Once upon a time, War was the domain of Ares.

Ares is a rather nasty god. War, under Ares, is full of rape, and torture, and genocide, and brutality. Under Ares, that's the whole POINT. You crush your enemy, see them driven before you, and laminate their women.

...

Different cultures have tried various methods to seal Ares away behind wards of honor, respect, and decency. When war "has to be" waged, we would prefer it to be waged in a way that minimizes the collateral damage - if for no other reason than that the victors will want to possess all of the resources that Ares would otherwise destroy.

Various forms of technology - both "soft" / cultural technology and "hard" / material technology - are used to construct the wards that keep various gods sealed away. Some of them work reasonably well; some work less well.

When you see the gods for what they are - vast collections of incentives and biases and strategies, interacting together to create a convincing appearance of independent decision-making - it starts to become clearer why some methods of sealing them away are more effective than others.

But the details are still very tricky to track.
